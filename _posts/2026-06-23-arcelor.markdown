## The Problem: When Milliseconds Mean Lives

In a steel mill, a *cobble* is a sudden, violent event where hot metal breaks out of the rolling process and flies unpredictably through the plant. For field workers nearby, these events are **life‑threatening**.

Traditional monitoring relies on human observation, which has critical flaws:

- **Reactive** — by the time an operator spots the cobble, it's often too late
- **High‑risk** — workers must be physically close to the line to see it
- **Inconsistent** — dependent on vigilance, fatigue, and line‑of‑sight

ArcelorMittal's **zero‑fatality safety goal** demanded an automated solution that could work with **existing infrastructure** — no new sensors, no expensive GPUs.

## The Solution: A Hybrid CV + Deep Learning Pipeline

> *"I designed a system that balances speed and accuracy — running 5 cameras in parallel on a CPU‑only industrial computer, with a 0.5‑second alert latency."*

I **led this project end‑to‑end**, collaborating closely with the Head of Technical Service and automation engineers (PLC specialists) to align the AI pipeline with factory constraints and existing control systems.

### Key Architectural Decisions

**1. ROS2 as the Orchestration Backbone**

ROS2 provided the modular, multi‑threaded architecture needed to process **5 camera streams in parallel** — each on its own thread, achieving **10 fps per stream** with sub‑second latency.

**2. A Three‑Stage Detection Pipeline**

| Stage | Technology | Purpose |
|-------|------------|---------|
| **Line Detection** | YOLOv8 | Identifies the rolling mill area of interest |
| **Anomaly Detection** | HSV filtering + KNN background subtractor | Highlights unexpected movement or objects in the line |
| **False Alarm Filtering** | ResNet classifier | Distinguishes real cobbles from benign anomalies (steam, lighting changes, scale drops) |

**3. CPU‑Only Deployment**

The entire pipeline runs on **standard industrial computers** with no GPU acceleration — a hard requirement from the plant's IT constraints.

**4. Instant Alerting**

When a real cobble is confirmed, an **instant alert** is sent to the control room and field workers via audible and visual signals.

## Pipeline Overview

```
5× Security Cameras (RTSP)
    ↓
ROS2 Nodes (Multi‑threaded, 1 per camera)
    ↓
YOLOv8 → Production Line Detection
    ↓
HSV + KNN → Anomaly Detection
    ↓
ResNet → False Alarm Filtering
    ↓
Alert Trigger → Control Room + Field Workers
```

> *"Each camera stream is processed independently on its own thread, ensuring that a bottleneck in one stream never blocks the others."*

## Key Lessons Learned

This project reinforced that **industrial AI is as much about integration as it is about algorithms**.

1. **Constraints drive innovation:** Forcing a "CPU‑only" rule pushed me to optimise the pipeline ruthlessly — YOLOv8 for localisation, classical CV for anomaly detection, and a lightweight ResNet for classification.
2. **ROS2 is a game‑changer for vision pipelines:** Its multi‑threaded, topic‑based architecture made it trivial to scale from 1 to 5 cameras without rewriting core logic.
3. **Domain expertise is non‑negotiable:** Working alongside automation engineers and safety officers was essential to understanding what a "real" cobble looks like versus a false positive.
4. **False positives kill trust:** A system that cries wolf too often gets ignored. The ResNet classifier was critical to maintaining operator confidence.
