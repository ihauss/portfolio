## The Problem: A Silent Threat to Global Food Security

Cassava is a vital crop across Africa, Asia, and South America — providing a staple food source for over 800 million people. However, the plant is highly susceptible to viral diseases that can devastate yields and threaten livelihoods.

**The Core Challenges:**

- **Five disease classes** to distinguish, some with visually similar symptoms:
  - Cassava Bacterial Blight (CBB)
  - Cassava Brown Streak Disease (CBSD)
  - Cassava Green Mottle (CGM)
  - Cassava Mosaic Disease (CMD)
  - Healthy

- **Limited labelled data** — agricultural datasets are often small and imbalanced
- **Deployment constraints** — any practical solution must run on modest hardware in resource‑constrained settings
- **Early detection is critical** — the difference between a treatable outbreak and total crop loss is a matter of days

---

## The Solution: A Hybrid Ensemble Approach

> *"Rather than betting on a single model, I combined a large Transformer with a lightweight CNN — getting the best of both worlds."*

### Methodology

The workflow was structured into three clear phases:

**1. Data Preprocessing**
Images were resized, augmented, and normalised using `torchvision.transforms` — applying random flips, rotations, and colour jitter to artificially expand the dataset and improve generalisation.

**2. Dual Model Architecture**
I trained two fundamentally different models in parallel:

| Model | Strengths |
|-------|-----------|
| **Vision Transformer (ViT)** | Captures long‑range spatial dependencies and global context |
| **MobileNetV3** | Lightweight, fast, and resilient to overfitting on small datasets |

**3. Ensemble Averaging**
The final prediction was computed by **averaging the softmax outputs** of both models. This ensemble approach:
- Mitigated overfitting from either individual model
- Benefited from both the ViT's representational power and MobileNetV3's efficiency
- Demonstrated **improved generalisation** on the validation set compared to either model alone

### Training Configuration

- **Loss function:** Cross‑entropy loss
- **Learning rate scheduling:** CosineAnnealingLR with configurable `T_max`
- **Validation:** Accuracy evaluated on a separate held‑out validation set

## Key Lessons Learned

This project taught me that **in agricultural AI, robustness matters more than peak accuracy**.

1. **Ensembles are underrated:** Averaging predictions from a large Transformer and a lightweight CNN gave better generalisation than either model alone — a simple trick that works surprisingly well.
2. **Data augmentation is non‑negotiable:** With limited labelled data, aggressive augmentation was essential to prevent overfitting and teach the model to recognise diseases across different lighting, angles, and leaf conditions.
3. **Know your deployment constraints:** MobileNetV3 wasn't just a backup — it was a deliberate choice to ensure the pipeline could run on edge devices, not just in the cloud.
4. **Kaggle competitions are great sandboxes:** They force you to iterate fast, experiment with architectures, and learn what actually works — not just what looks good on paper.
